Mango and Rice

>

Ingredients

  • 1 cup glutinous rice (also known as sweet rice or sticky rice)
  • 1 ½ cups coconut milk
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 2 ripe mangoes, peeled, pitted, and sliced

Cooking Instructions

  1. Soak the Rice: Rinse the glutinous rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for at least 4 hours or overnight
  2. Steam the Rice: Drain the soaked rice and place it in a steamer lined with cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel. Steam the rice for about 25-30 minutes, or until it's tender and cooked through.
  3. Prepare the Coconut Sauce: In a saucepan, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly until the sugar dissolves. Do not boil.
  4. Combine Rice and Coconut Sauce: Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl. Pour 1 cup of the coconut sauce over the rice and gently fold it in, allowing the rice to absorb the coconut mixture. Let it sit for a few minutes to absorb the flavors.
  5. Assemble the Dish: Serve the sticky rice on a plate or in bowls. Arrange sliced mangoes on the side or on top of the rice.
  6. Garnish: Drizzle additional coconut sauce over the top. Optionally, sprinkle sesame seeds and garnish with mint leaves for a pop of color and extra flavor.
  7. Serve: Serve the Mango Sticky Rice warm or at room temperature.
