Gulab Jamun

>

Ingredients

  • 1 cup khoya (mawa) or milk solids
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
  • A pinch of baking soda
  • 2-3 tablespoons milk (as needed to form a smooth dough)
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying

Cooking Instructions

  1. Prepare the Gulab Jamun Dough: In a mixing bowl, crumble the khoya to remove any lumps. Add all-purpose flour, ghee, and a pinch of baking soda. Mix well. Gradually add milk and knead to form a smooth, soft dough. Be careful not to over-knead.
  2. Shape the Gulab Jamun Balls: Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth balls, ensuring there are no cracks. Make sure the balls are uniform in size for even cooking.
  3. Fry the Gulab Jamun: Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, reduce the heat to low-medium. Fry the Gulab Jamun balls until they are golden brown, turning them gently for even cooking. Remove them from the oil and drain on a paper towel.
  4. Soak in Sugar Syrup: While the Gulab Jamun balls are still warm, immerse them in the warm sugar syrup. Allow them to soak for at least 1-2 hours so that they absorb the syrup and become soft and sweet.
  5. Serve: Garnish with chopped nuts if desired. Serve Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature.
